Past Projects  
1. Old Age Home Visit

3. Independence Day celebrations


We visited our adopted government school at Chunchungatta on the occasion of Independence Day. The event was celebrated with fun fare and saw some great performances by the school kids. Some of the friends of Rotaractors also entertained the kids by giving a dance performance. This was followed by distribution of awards for class toppers and provision of books and sweets for all the school kids.
4. Notebook distribution at four Government Schools at Hoskote


We visited various schools in rural Bangalore in search of a school that really need our attention and care. We visited about 4 schools in Kolar Road, which needed immense help from us in terms of infrastructure such as building roofs and toilets. We decided to get these things to one such school in Nimbekaipura as a beginning and are working towards raising funds for this cause. In all we visited 4 schools and distributed notebooks and chocolates.
5. Tree Plantation Drive at Tiruhalli


We planted around 200 saplings in Turuhalli. It was a great experience for about 15 Rotaractors and their friends enthusiastically digging and planting seedlings. This created a lot of awareness among public watching us do all the work and also got a sense of encouragement for all of us to do something towards the environment. This project was completed with the support of Rotaract District 3190.

7. Donation of Mats, groceries at Old Age and MR home


We visited an old age home near vasanthpura where there are close to 60 MR and old people resident. We in association with GE Healthcare organized the visit to this place. GE donated about 60 mats for the MR patients to sleep for the coming winter. We also provided food for all the inmates in the place. We also bought groceries and disinfectants for the place. We sang and danced with the inmates trying to make a difference in their lives.
8. Inauguration of Solar water Heater at the Old Age home


One of our flagship projects is our association with a home for Old Age and Mentally challenged people called Sri Ambhighara Chowdaiah Educational Society, Vasanthpura for 2 years. As a step forward in providing support and love to them, we have successfully installed a "Solar Water Heater" at their premises so that they are supplied with hot water round the clock. We have built this with the support of HP SNIL and Rotary StillWater, USA .

14. Medical Camp for children


On 9th of March, a free medical camp was conducted by Rotaract Club of Jayanagar and Rotary club of Jayanagar, RI Dist. 3190 at St. Anthony's Kannada Higher Primary School, Jayanagar 4th T Block, Tilaknagar from 9:00 AM till 2:00 PM. Four Doctors with an ANM have attended the camp. More than 500 people got benefited making the camp a grand success, including 450 school children and 53 women were screened. 16. Learning through Play

17. Artificial Limb Donation


It was yet another day which brought smiles on the faces of physically challenged people who are facing locomotor disabilities, as Rotaract Club of Jayanagar and Rotary Club of Bangalore, Jayanagar, in association with Sri Mahaveer Jain Sahayatha Samithi and Sri Mahavir Jain Hospital held an Artificial Limb donation program for the third successful year. In all, donations were made for 127 limbs, out of which Rotaract Club of Jayanagar donated Rupees 1 Lakh and Rotary Club of Jayanagar donated Rupees 27 thousand.
18. Environment drives… Save Ucchangi hills


We went to Ucchangi hills in Shakleshpur district to stage a protest against the mafia who is illegally mining and chopping down trees at a very scenic and a trekker’s paradise… Ucchangi hills. This was an initiative by an ex Rotaractor or Jayanagar Rtr. Sampath who has an NGO namely “Sharavati Adventure Trails”. We put a lot of effort to gather many IT professionals and locals of Ucchangi to be a part of this protest and invited many press and print media to cover the same.
19. The Library Project


We established multi-lingual libraries in St.Antony's Kannada Higher Primary School and Govt. Primary School,Chunchghatta. The schools are provided with colorful English books and local language books along with safe and accessible storage place for the books, so that the children feel comfortable to access the books and nurture their educational curiosities. The library is supervised by a trained librarian, to guide the school staff and children about the precise usage of the books. The entire library scheme is taken up as a stage development program for the kids, where the language improvement of the kids is made calculative and traceable by the librarian through different charts, cards and posters.
20. Trip to Mulayangiri


Rotractors of RCB Jayanagar went for an adventure trek to MULAYAN GIRI; the highest peak in Karnataka on 16 January 2009. Situated at 6,317 feet high from Sea Level, the place is 12 Kms away from Chickmagalur. We had a fun time climbing the 400 staircase which lead us to the peak and served us with a view to cherish for. We were shrinking away to glory because of the early morning breeze and a sweet tinge of sunlight from here and there, which kept us going. A visit to Anaconda falls on our way back was no less than a dessert after an unmemorable time spent.
